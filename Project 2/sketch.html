The Way
Leyendecker
sketches

Before meeting a model, he drew prolific sketches. After he brought the model, he made rough sketches in charcoal and pencil.

“One can often work directly from the model, whereas a cover requires more careful planning and should be designed to fill a given space on the order of a decoration."

"My first step is to fill a sketchpad with a number of small rough sketches about two by three inches, keeping them on one sheet so you can compare them at a glance. Select the one that seems to tell the story most clearly and adding more detail and color as needed."

When a sketch suited him, Leyendecker drew a grid on the final canvas so that the composition of scale can stay consistent. Leyendecker referred this process as “picture puzzle.”
