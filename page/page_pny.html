Resource Center

Redesigning resource center page of pnypartners.com

Issue

In the previous design, people had hard time finding the files they were looking for.

Criteria
  • Target User: PNY Partners, PNY Sales Team.
  • Availability: Filters were possible to be added.
  • Things to keep in mind:
    Making sure there is a visible difference between category and file.
    Making them simple and obvious.
  • Reference:Document Management System (Ex: Sharepoint, Hubspot)
File Organization

There were many different types of files but mainly two ways of organizing files were possible: by the type of asset and type of product it was about.


Wireframe

We created multiple examples of wireframes and narrowed down to two directions: One with the filters and other with the combination of sidebar.I focused on the combination of sidebar.


Prototypes
  • After review, the wireframe with the sidebar received most positive comments, so we developed prototype based on it.
  • Narrowed down to three main designs using slightly different color and different styles of filters.
  • Started thinking about how it works on responsive design - especially in mobile.

Final Prototypes
  • Category based on asset type
  • Filters based on the product type
  • Filters based on file type
  • Can be sorted by ID, type and title
  • Download icon shows up when hover

Contact Page: Pny Pro Support

Redesigning “Contact Us” page for Pro Side


About
  • There were three different “Contact Us” pages.
  • What I focused was the “Contact Us” page for PNY pro products and rename it “PNY Pro Support.”
Issue
  • Long form: too much scroll.
  • “Contact Us” link was inside “Contact Us” page.

Criteria
  • New CTA Buttons needed to be added: One for submitting form and the other for email to gopny@pny.com
  • Design: Should be different from the rest of the “Contact Us” Page
Sketch
  • Referenced different types of contact and support pages.
  • Different color schemes that differentiate from other contact pages
  • Different designs for making CTA buttons show better than other information on the page.

Prototypes
  • Narrowed down to 5 different designs, including light and dark versions.
  • Form is redesigned as pop-up.

Final Prototype
  • Dark Background
  • Additional CTA Buttons
  • Form pop-up
  • No “Contact Us” Link

Final Outcome

Conclusion

What I learned
  • How to make each website page serve its specific function - form follows function.
  • How to make sure the design is consistent with other elements.
  • How to apply comments from different people to the design.

Thank you!
